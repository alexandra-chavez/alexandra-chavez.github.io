I am excited to share that our new article is now available in <i>Animals</i>!

In this study, we assessed the northernmost population of the endangered huemul deer, <i>Hippocamelus bisulcus</i>, using DNA obtained non-invasively from fecal samples collected in the wild.

We found that the population retains moderate genetic variation but has low allelic richness and a small effective population size. Our demographic simulations also predict a continued loss of genetic diversity under all the scenarios we explored.

These findings provide important genetic information for designing conservation and management plans aimed at protecting this rare and elusive South American deer.
